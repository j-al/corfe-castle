About the Castle

The castle has had a long history since its completion in 1107.

It was built on a steep hill in the village now known as Corfe Castle, in the county of Dorset, England. Unlike other castles of its time, it was built out of stone, rather than wood, indicating its high status.

In 1645, as a result of the English Civil War, the castle was demolished, and it has remained in this ruined state to the present day.

Today, the castle provides excellent hilltop views of the surrounding Dorset countryside, and is a fun, educational day out for those of all ages.
